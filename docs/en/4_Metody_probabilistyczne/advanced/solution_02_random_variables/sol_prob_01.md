## Task 1

A test was conducted in a student group. Let **X** denote the grade (on the grading scale 2–5) of a randomly selected student.

### Is X a random variable?

Yes, **X is a random variable**.

A random variable is a function that assigns a numerical value to the outcome of a random experiment. In this case, the experiment is **randomly selecting one student from the group** and observing their grade.

Since the possible grades are from the set {2, 3, 4, 5}, **X is a discrete random variable**.

---

## Definition of the Random Variable

Let:

X = grade of a randomly selected student

Possible values of **X**:

X ∈ {2, 3, 4, 5}

---

## Given Grades

The grades of the 10 students are:

5, 4, 3, 3, 4, 5, 3, 3, 4, 2

Total number of students:

n = 10

---

## Step 1: Count Each Grade

| Grade | Number of Students |
|------|--------------------|
| 2 | 1 |
| 3 | 4 |
| 4 | 3 |
| 5 | 2 |

---

## Step 2: Calculate Probabilities

The probability of each grade is calculated using:

P(X = x) = (number of students with grade x) / 10

| Grade (X) | Probability |
|-----------|-------------|
| 2 | 1/10 = 0.1 |
| 3 | 4/10 = 0.4 |
| 4 | 3/10 = 0.3 |
| 5 | 2/10 = 0.2 |

---

## Probability Distribution of X

| X | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| P(X) | 0.1 | 0.4 | 0.3 | 0.2 |

---

## Conclusion

- **X is a discrete random variable** representing the grade of a randomly selected student.
- The probability distribution is obtained by dividing the number of occurrences of each grade by the total number of students (10).

## Task 8

Two people from city A are trying to establish a telephone connection with city B.

Let:

- **X** denote the number of attempts made by the first person
- **Y** denote the number of attempts made by the second person

It is assumed that the attempts are **independent**.

The probability distributions are:

For person 1:

P(X = 1) = 0.6  
P(X = 2) = 0.4  

For person 2:

P(Y = 1) = 0.5  
P(Y = 2) = 0.5  

We need to determine the **joint distribution of (X,Y)**.

---

## Step 1: Use Independence

Since the variables are independent:

P(X = x, Y = y) = P(X = x) · P(Y = y)

---

## Step 2: Compute Joint Probabilities

### P(X = 1, Y = 1)

P = 0.6 × 0.5 = 0.30

### P(X = 1, Y = 2)

P = 0.6 × 0.5 = 0.30

### P(X = 2, Y = 1)

P = 0.4 × 0.5 = 0.20

### P(X = 2, Y = 2)

P = 0.4 × 0.5 = 0.20

---

## Step 3: Joint Distribution Table

| X \ Y | 1 | 2 |
|------|---|---|
| 1 | 0.30 | 0.30 |
| 2 | 0.20 | 0.20 |

---

## Final Result

The joint distribution of the two-dimensional random variable **(X,Y)** is:

| X \ Y | 1 | 2 |
|------|---|---|
| 1 | 0.30 | 0.30 |
| 2 | 0.20 | 0.20 |

All probabilities sum to:

0.30 + 0.30 + 0.20 + 0.20 = 1

## Task 9

A product is produced by **three plants**.  
The probabilities that each plant produces a **first-quality item** are:

P(F₁) = 0.97  
P(F₂) = 0.90  
P(F₃) = 0.86  

Three items are taken — **one from each plant**.  
Then **one of these three items is chosen randomly**.

We need the probability that the selected item is **first quality**.

---

### Step 1: Probability that the chosen item comes from each plant

Since one of the three items is selected randomly:

P(Plant₁) = 1/3  
P(Plant₂) = 1/3  
P(Plant₃) = 1/3  

---

### Step 2: Use the total probability formula

Let **F** be the event that the chosen item is first quality.

P(F) = P(Plant₁)·P(F₁) + P(Plant₂)·P(F₂) + P(Plant₃)·P(F₃)

Substitute the values:

P(F) = (1/3)(0.97) + (1/3)(0.90) + (1/3)(0.86)

P(F) = (0.97 + 0.90 + 0.86) / 3

P(F) = 2.73 / 3

**P(F) = 0.91**

---

## Final Result

**The probability that the randomly chosen item is first quality is:**

**P(F) = 0.91**

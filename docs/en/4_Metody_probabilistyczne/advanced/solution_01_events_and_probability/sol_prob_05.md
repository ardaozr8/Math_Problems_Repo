## Task 5

Let:
- A = event that the water volume is in the interval **⟨125, 250⟩**
- B = event that the water volume is in the interval **⟨200, 300⟩**

Given:
- **P(A) = 0.6**
- **P(B) = 0.7**
- **P(A ∪ B) = 0.8**

---

### 1. Complementary event \(A'\)

The complement of an event is calculated as:

**P(A') = 1 - P(A)**

Substitute the value:

**P(A') = 1 - 0.6 = 0.4**

---

### 2. Intersection \(A ∩ B\)

Using the formula for the union of two events:

**P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A ∩ B)**

Substitute the known values:

**0.8 = 0.6 + 0.7 - P(A ∩ B)**

**0.8 = 1.3 - P(A ∩ B)**

**P(A ∩ B) = 1.3 - 0.8**

**P(A ∩ B) = 0.5**

---

### 3. Probability \(P(A' ∩ B')\)

This means the water volume **does not belong to either interval**.

Using the complement rule:

**P(A' ∩ B') = 1 - P(A ∪ B)**

Substitute the value:

**P(A' ∩ B') = 1 - 0.8**

**P(A' ∩ B') = 0.2**

---

## Final Results

1. **P(A') = 0.4**  
2. **P(A ∩ B) = 0.5**  
3. **P(A' ∩ B') = 0.2**

## Task 8

The coded information contains **7 pulses** in total:

- **4 pulses of type A**
- **2 pulses of type B**
- **1 pulse of type C**

The pulses are arranged randomly.

---

### 1. Probability that the first received pulse will be A

Since there are 4 pulses of type **A** out of 7 total pulses:

**P(first pulse is A) = 4/7**

---

### 2. Probability that the first received pulse will be A or C

There are:
- 4 pulses of type **A**
- 1 pulse of type **C**

So there are **5 favorable pulses** out of 7 total pulses.

**P(first pulse is A or C) = (4 + 1)/7 = 5/7**

---

### 3. Probability that the first two pulses will be A and C in that order

We calculate this step by step:

- Probability that the **first pulse is A**: **4/7**
- After removing one A, there are **6 pulses left**, including **1 pulse of C**
- Probability that the **second pulse is C**: **1/6**

Now multiply:

**P(first two pulses are A and C) = (4/7) × (1/6) = 4/42 = 2/21**

---

## Final Results

1. **P(first pulse is A) = 4/7**  
2. **P(first pulse is A or C) = 5/7**  
3. **P(first two pulses are A and C in that order) = 2/21**
